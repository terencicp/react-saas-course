# Chapter 3.7 prerequisites review

## Missing prerequisites

- **Lesson 3.7.1** — `Uint8Array` construction and the typed-array model. Quote: "allocate `new Uint8Array(32)`, fill, base64url-encode to a string" and "the 2026 reflex on every byte-or-buffer call is the `Uint8Array` over `ArrayBuffer`". The typed-array model (`Uint8Array`, `ArrayBuffer`, allocation, and manipulation) is never introduced in any prior chapter. Chapter 3.6.2 mentions `Uint8Array` only as the chunk type yielded by `ReadableStream` iteration — it does not teach construction, the `ArrayBuffer` relationship, or the broader typed-array mental model that Lesson 3.7.1 builds its entire HMAC flow on. Suggested source: new lesson in Unit 2 (e.g., 2.3 container additions) or an explicit primer at the top of Chapter 3.7 itself. Severity: **high**.

- **Lesson 3.7.1** — `TextEncoder` / `TextDecoder` as the string-to-bytes conversion primitive. Quote: "`TextEncoder` to turn the secret and the payload into `Uint8Array` bytes" and "`TextEncoder` / `TextDecoder` as the only string-to-bytes conversion the course endorses (no `atob` / `btoa` outside base64 corner cases)". Chapter 3.6.2 uses `TextEncoder` inside a streaming code snippet and `TextDecoder` with `{ stream: true }` for chunk decoding, but neither is introduced as a standalone API with its construction shape, call signature, or encode/decode semantics. Lesson 3.7.1 uses both as known primitives from the first bullet. Suggested source: 3.6.2 (expand to explicitly introduce `TextEncoder`/`TextDecoder` as the string↔bytes bridge) or new lesson in Unit 3. Severity: **high**.

- **Lesson 3.7.1** — `base64url` encoding as a string representation of raw bytes. Quote: "allocate `new Uint8Array(32)`, fill, base64url-encode to a string". No prior lesson introduces base64 or base64url encoding: the distinction from standard base64, the `atob`/`btoa` legacy surface, or a modern alternative. The chapter framing notes "`no atob / btoa` outside base64 corner cases" which implies the student already knows what these are. Suggested source: 3.7.1 (teach inline as a one-liner pattern) or 3.6.2 (add alongside `TextEncoder`). Severity: **medium**.
